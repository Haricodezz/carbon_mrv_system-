About the Platform

What is Carbon MRV Registry?

The Carbon MRV Registry connects forests to financial markets using satellite data, AI, and blockchain. Landowners earn carbon credits automatically; companies buy them for ESG compliance.

🛰️

Satellite Monitoring (MRV)

Sentinel-2 satellite imagery is processed every month using XGBoost ML models to measure biomass and CO₂ sequestration. No manual field surveys needed — automated, verifiable, tamper-proof.

⛓️

Blockchain-Minted Tokens

Every carbon credit is minted as a CCT token on the Polygon blockchain. This creates an immutable, auditable record of carbon stock, credit issuance, and retirement — visible to all parties.

🌾

Empowering Landowners

Farmers, NGOs, and forest communities can register their land for free. Credits are minted directly to their MetaMask wallet, enabling a new revenue stream from sustainable land stewardship.

🏢

Enterprise Carbon Compliance

Companies purchase verified CCT credits through the marketplace and retire them to offset emissions. Government-ready PDF certificates with QR blockchain verification are issued instantly.

What is CCT?

CCT is the native carbon credit token of this platform, minted on the Polygon blockchain. Each 1 CCT represents 1 tonne of verified CO₂ sequestration.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the platform and carbon credits.

A carbon credit represents 1 tonne of CO₂ that has been sequestered (absorbed) from the atmosphere. On this platform, each CCT token equals 1 tonne of verified CO₂ sequestration measured by satellite. Companies buy credits to offset their own emissions, effectively making their operations carbon neutral.

Credits are issued based on the increase in carbon stock on your registered land, measured monthly via Sentinel-2 satellite imagery and our XGBoost ML model. The more forest growth on your land, the more credits you earn. 80% of measured credits go to your wallet; 20% is held in a buffer pool as insurance.

The system automatically scans your land coordinates using Sentinel-2 satellite data each month. It extracts vegetation indices (NDVI, EVI) and uses a trained XGBoost model to predict biomass and CO₂ stock. The change in stock from month to month determines how many credits are issued. Auditors can review and approve these results before credits are minted.

Organizations register and are verified on the platform. They then access the marketplace (visible only after login) to browse active listings and purchase CCT credits. Credits are stored in their Virtual Vault. When ready to claim offsetting, they retire the credits — which burns the tokens on-chain and generates a PDF retirement certificate with a QR code for blockchain verification.

Currently the platform runs on the Polygon Amoy Testnet — a test network for development and validation. This allows full blockchain functionality (minting, transfers, retirement) without real financial risk. Migration to Polygon mainnet is planned for production launch.

The buffer pool is a system reserve that holds 20% of all minted carbon credits. It acts as an insurance fund — if a project is destroyed (fire, deforestation), the buffer pool can compensate buyers rather than invalidating their credits. This protects the integrity of the carbon market.

Landowners need a MetaMask wallet to receive CCT tokens on the Polygon network. Organizations purchasing credits need a registered wallet address for the transaction. If you're new to MetaMask, it's a free browser extension — visit metamask.io to get started. No real cryptocurrency is needed on the testnet.
